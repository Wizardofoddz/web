A few coins with which our team has been involved:

  • Florincoin
    (FLO)

    Florincoin (FLO) is a scrypt proof of work crypto-currency with a 40 second block time. It is based on the Bitcoin and Litecoin source code, but introduces a transaction comments feature. These transaction comments enable Florincoin to facilitate a number of decentralized applications. Proof of publication, peer to peer gaming, no pre-mine, and 1 block difficulty retargeting all set this coin apart.

  • Razor
    (RZR)

    Razor capitalizes on the brilliant technology of Neutrinocoin, but was launched in a fair and open manner which provides a distinct market advantage. It implements the Tor network natively which has been identified by independent researchers as the key to true anonymity when using cryptocurrencies. The coin economics themselves lend towards a high market price making it suitable for everyday commerce applications at current levels of cryptocurrency acceptance. The design also provides for the ability to institute a proof-of-stake system in addition to the proof-of-work system in order to provide for inflation necessary for increased acceptance.

  • Dirac
    (XDQ)

    Dirac has become the economic anchor for the Blake ecosystem and a coin built specifically for a long term trading use case. The design capitalizes on the lessons learned with Unobtanium. The economics are quite similar to UNO, but a partnership with the exchange AllCrypt enabled for a special pairing against Brian Kelly’s NautiliusCoin, a coin designed for financial stability. Dirac is also traded on Bittrex Exchange against Bitcoin, which provides for the most profitable, consistent arbitrage dynamics of any coin on the market.



  • OpenSourceCoin
    (OSC)

    A clone of Peercoin (including centralized checkpointing) that provides for micro-finance applications. It is a well-vetted coin that is almost a year old; has survived many attacks and can easily utilize the Peercoin developed infrastructure. Economics will be redesigned in June 2014 to increase market value where it is use-case suitable.

  • Unobtanium
    (UNO)

    One of the oldest and most respected coins on the market, UNO boasts a maximum coin total of 250,000 coins which as of June 2014 have almost expired. It’s considered a true “trader’s coin” in that the market liquidity dries up very quickly and the price rises when someone buys into the market, providing almost cyclical periods of stability and volatility making it incredibly attractive to own mid to long term.

  • PopularCoin
    (POP)

    PopularCoin is a coin designed for a specific use case, popular consumption. Both the marketing and the economic design are pre-disposed towards common acceptance as a one-size-fits-all loyalty rewards network for the entertainment industry.



  • PetroDollar
    (XPD)

    PetroDollar is a mathematical simulation of the oil market utilizing the comparative similarities between the production of crude oil, the market that sells it, the production of cryptocurrencies, and the markets that trade them. The key feature is the “crisis model” at which point supply is reduced by 3000% to simulate the 1970’s oil crisis, and a parabolic destructive fee system is instituted which has been independently calculated by market analysts to co-incide with the approach of peak oil in the next 40 years.

  • AmKoin
    (AmK)

    It has 23 total coins with “Dead Eye Rewards,” meaning you never miss them. The network employs a 7 minute block time, Kimoto’s Gravity Well (the first SHA-256 coin to do so), and a non-reducing proof-of-work. All of which combines for a steady stream of coins generated for the next five years and then proof-of-work ends. Recent advancements in the market such as the addition of proof-of-stake give this rare coin a potentially inflationary future component.
